About Me

Here's a little bit about me

My name is Arush Shah and I'm currently a Sophomore majoring in Computer Engineering at the University of Maryland, College Park. I have a keen interest in software development, hardware, and finance. Outside of academics, I like to strum on a guitar or play a rigorous game of table tennis.



Here's a quick overview of my experience

  • June 2018

    Northrop Grumman

    Cybersecurity Software Engineering Intern

    I spent my freshman year summer at Northrop Grumman. I was given the opportunity to work on a research and development team to develop software tools for the U.S. Army - Psychological Operations. I spent most of my time here prototyping application features using Pega Systems for the core development team.

  • 2017

    University of Maryland - College Park

    Bachelor's Degree: Computer Engineering
    Minor: Cybersecurity
    GPA: 3.9/4.0

  • 2016

    BrightLogic

    Software Developer

    I spent part of my senior year in high school interning at BrightLogic as a software developer. I developed Unity-based applications for an optical sensor integrated interactive LED floor. I also created an Arduino-based IoT device communication interface using MQTT subscription/publication protocol. My main project, however, was implementing a database replication system using Python to facilitate production and reporting database maintenance for a digital beverage ordering system for casinos.

  • 2016

    Maker Depot

    Engineering Intern

    I spent the summer of my Junior year interning at Maker Depot. Here, I developed a Python based curriculum management software to promote STEM in public schools. I also spent a lot of time teaching classes on 3D printing, Arduino, and Python programming.
